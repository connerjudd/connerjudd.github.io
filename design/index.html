Design Principles

Hick's Law

Amazon

Amazon does a great job in assisting the user to find what they want or where they are trynig to go. In mobile browsing they remove a lot of "clutter" by hiding menu items in an icon and making an easy visual map of the page. The design is clean and easy to follow.

Fitt's Law

Google

I believe the google home page to be a perfect example of Fitt's Law. Partly, becuase of the simplicity of the site, but it follows Fitt's law regardless. The search bar is large and when the site loads your eye is immediately drawn to it. I believe the size of the search bar is spot on. It is big enough that you are drawn to it with ease. It also follows the rule of thirds principle.

Rule of Thirds

Reebok

What I like most about this site is how responsive the site is. The large view displays a photo of two shoes and the image follows the rule of thirds methodology. When the screen shrinks they crop they original image to display what is really important.
