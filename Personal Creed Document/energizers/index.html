Energizers

Influential People in my Life

Cami Judd

Cami is my wife. We have been married for almost 9 years. We have been through a lot! And through thick and thin she has supported me and help me raise our two wonderful daughters. I know she loves me. I know she likes me. And she is my best friend.

Jennifer Judd

Jennifer is my Mom. I was a Dennis the Menace as a child and the reason I am alive is a testament to her care and perseverance. She has been my best teacher, friend, therapist, and shoulder to cry on. She taught me to drive a stick shift in San Francisco this woman has celestial patience.

Darin Judd

Darin is my Dad. My Dad is amazing. He has been a model of hard work, dedication, and family loyalty. He has been there for my hardest times and is always my first call when I need advice. I am blessed to have the relationship with him.

Neil Andersen

Neil Andersen is my father-in-law. Many people do not have a good relationship with their in-laws. That is not my case. I have had the opportunity to really get to know my in-laws and I truly enjoy their company. I worked with him and we really bonded, and he was a huge support during the most difficult time in my life.

Gay Andersen

Gay is my mother-in-law. Gay is loving, kind, intelligent, and a great example. She leads by example, every day she is up early reading her scriptures at the kitchen table. She writes in her journal every day. She tries very hard to speak kindly of everyone and is the defender of the underdog.

Dick Judd

Dick Judd is my grandfather. Dick is one of the hardest working people I know. He brought his family our of poverty by the sweat of his brow. He worked for 35+ years stocking shelves for Nabisco at grocery stores all over the state of Utah. He would work 12-14 hour shifts 6 days a week. He sent all his sons on missions and supported those who wanted to go to college. He is fiercely loyal and loving.

Switch Points

Mission

Being somewhat of a wayward teen, having the childhood goal of a mission was always in the back of my mind. When I graduated high school, I knew that I had to make some adjustments in my life before I would be ready to go. With the help of my parents and my stake president I made the course corrections I needed to and received the call to serve the people of Sao Paulo, Brazil. There I learned the meaning of selfless service. How to love complete strangers and work hard.

Wedding

Deciding my wife was the woman who I was supposed to marry was easy. I had an overwhelming feeling of peace and comfort when I prayed to know. She has been a help mate, friend, counselor, and lover. I can honestly say she knows me better than anyone on this Earth.

Academy

Shortly after marriage I applied to the San Bernardino County Sheriff’s Department. I was accepted into the academy. It was hard. It was a stress induced environment. There were people assigned to make sure your time there was hard and unpleasant. It was for six months. I had one of the Tac-officers pull me into his office and he told me that he was not worried that I would make it. He said that I was capable. From that moment I knew I could do anything. It was a huge boost in my confidence come graduation.

Having Kids

Three weeks after I graduated, I was blessed with my daughter Carly Rose Judd. She changed my life for the better. She loves me and trust me in a way no one has before. I love her and she changed my life for the better.

Dealing with Negative Thoughts

  1. Look for the positive when you are surrounded by the negative.
  2. Do not put too much stock in other people’s opinions.
  3. When you have negative thoughts do not believe them. Learn to challenge them.
  4. Try to remove the negative influences that you can control.
  5. If you fail, it is ok. Pick yourself up and try again.
  6. Learn to talk through your trials.
  7. Work out.
  8. Don’t be ruled by your fears.
  9. Serve others.
  10. Show gratitude for those around me.
  11. Eat right.
  12. Forgive others.
